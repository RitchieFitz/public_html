Computer Science History

I started getting interested in Computer Science when I was about 12 years old. My cousin, Richard, and I started making games with GameMaker. It was a lot of fun as a kid, and I enjoyed watching others play the games I made. Since then my passion for learning more about computers has increased exponentially. I didn't really do much more than that since I was a kid till college.

At college I was trying to decide what to major in because I had a lot of different interests. Computers stuck out to me, and I decided to give it a try. My first class was the hardest. My class at first had 30-40 kids in it, but in the end it ended up being around 5-8. I learned a lot, but I really fell in love with it in my second class. After that I decided that that is what I needed to do. I now am getting my major in Computer Information Technologies and my minor in Computer Science. My first internship was with Barton Consulting, they build websites, it was fun to get into that realm more and now I can easily build a websites using many different tools. I also worked for AMD as a computer scientist, building a python GUI program to help them validate certain things. It was an awesome project and I was able to learn a lot.

Along the way I have been using and teaching myself many different languages, and I haven't used all of these in building projects but I have used all of them in coding before.
